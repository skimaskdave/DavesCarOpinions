2012 Skoda Citigo S

S in this case doesn't stand for sport unfortunately. I don't know what it officially stands for, but it is the base model of the pre-facelift Skoda Citigo. This car is my first car, and I love it. It doesn't come with central locking, electric windows or air conditioning but that doesn't matter, it is brilliant. I have modified it, I put a custom exhuast on it. Longlife Exhausts in Southampton did that for me. I also bought the Garmin PID device so I have a rev counter and coolant temperature gauage (it has satnav and can show what music is on and also bluetooth calls, but I don't care for any of that, I want to see how fast my engine is spinning and how hot it is). I did what any teenager would do when they like cars, blacked out the badges, put a sunstrip on it and some VRS grille stripes (all of these were eBay specials) but I enjoy modifying my car, and I also enjoyed it stock. It's perfect for going round town, it's tiny so fits through plenty of tight spaces, and into tight spaces when parking. - 23/03/2023

Update: I have modified it more. I have stuck a VRS badge on the interior (upbadging is cringe) but I bought the badges a year ago so I thought I would at least make the interior a little nicer. I have also added some footwell lights (which I did a few months ago now). I had two sets of alloy wheels (neither of which were on the car), so I have finally sold a set and now can hopefully afford to get the other set on the car. All I need to do is buy some tires. I know, not cheap. In addition to this, I have bought the stuff so I can install a kill switch, however I have not installed said switch yet because to get to the fuel pump relay I have to take the lower part of the dash off, which takes longer than wanted and I really don't want to fanny about with it. - 13/04/2023
